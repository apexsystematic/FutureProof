Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 2026  ·  Applies to all visitors worldwide

§ 1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work correctly, remember your preferences, and provide basic usage information to website operators.

This Cookie Policy explains exactly what cookies are used on www.futureproof.report, why, and how you can control them. We have deliberately kept our cookie usage to the absolute minimum necessary to operate the site.

§ 2 Cookies We Use

Currently, this Website only utilizes Strictly Necessary Cookies. These are required for the core functionality of the website, such as allowing our payment processor to securely process your transaction and prevent fraud, as well as essential security routing.

Because these cookies are strictly essential to provide the service you are requesting, we do not require prior consent to use them under the Spanish Law 34/2002 (LSSI-CE), and therefore you will not see a cookie consent banner on this site.

We do not use third-party marketing, tracking, or analytics cookies at this time. Should this change in the future, we will update this policy and implement a consent mechanism in full compliance with EU and Spanish laws.

§ 3 Essential vs Non-Essential Cookies

Under the EU ePrivacy Directive (and its national implementations, including Spain's LSSI) and UK PECR, cookies are categorised as:

  • Essential cookies — strictly necessary for the website to function. These do not require your consent. The Cloudflare security cookies listed above fall into this category.
  • Non-essential cookies — analytics, advertising, or preference cookies that are not strictly necessary. These require your prior consent. We currently use none of these.

The Google Fonts cookie occupies a grey area — it is a functional dependency of the site's design, though not strictly essential to its operation. We are reviewing self-hosting our fonts to eliminate this entirely. Until then, it is disclosed here in full.

§ 4 How to Control Cookies

Because we use only essential and minimal functional cookies, we do not operate a cookie consent banner — essential cookies are exempt from consent requirements under EU and UK law.

However, you can control cookies at the browser level at any time:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

Please note that blocking essential cookies may affect the security and functionality of the site. Blocking Google Fonts cookies may cause fonts to fall back to your system default, which will affect the visual appearance of the site but not its functionality.

You can also opt out of Google's use of data by visiting Google's partner sites policy.

§ 5 Third-Party Services

When you submit an order form or make a payment, you will interact with third-party services (such as our payment processor) that operate under their own cookie and privacy policies. We have no control over cookies set by these third parties, and recommend you review their policies before proceeding.

Links to relevant third-party policies will be provided in your order confirmation email.

§ 6 Changes to This Policy

If our cookie usage changes — for example, if we introduce analytics — we will update this page and, where required by law, implement a consent mechanism before setting any new non-essential cookies. We encourage you to check this page periodically.
